The 1980s saw the rise of many different things. One of the of the industry's first was publishing houses such as Sony, Apple, Microsoft, and Activision-Blizzard

Besides the publishing houseses, this era also saw the small beginnings of online gaming and handheld lcd games. Though this was great and all, there was soon a "crash" of the video game industry that on one hand bankrupted several companies but led to the popularity of Japanese companies Nintendo and Sega

After the crash, soon the third and fourth generation of consoles came such as the Nintendo Entertainment System (NES), SG-1000, Sega Genesis, and Super NES. These has led to famous franchises such as The Legend of Zelda, Dragon Quest, Phantasy Star, and Final Fantasy.

The 90's brought many innovations in the gaming industry. There was a transition to 3D graphics and led to several genres such as first-person shooters, real-time strategy, and MMOS.

First mentioned in the Golden Age page, arcades and arcades games made a suprising comeback. Afterwards, Nintendo released the Game Boy and its succesors, the Game Boy Pocket, Color, and Advance. Besides handhelds, PC Gaming saw a boost in hardware and and saw a rise of small developers such as Apogee, Epic MegaGames, Maxis, and id Software.
